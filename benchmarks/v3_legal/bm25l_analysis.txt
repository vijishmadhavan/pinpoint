BM25L vs FTS5 on v3 legal subset
Date: 2026-03-15
Dataset: benchmarks/v3_legal/expanded_queries.json
Corpus: benchmarks/v3_legal/subset_corpus (500 files)
Report: benchmarks/v3_legal/bm25l_report.json

Headline:
- FTS5 remains best for top-1 precision.
- BM25L improves recall and slightly improves NDCG, but hurts success@1.
- FTS + BM25L RRF is the best overall lexical blend so far, but it still does not beat FTS5 on top-1.

Metrics:
- fts_only: s@1 0.3067, MRR 0.3438, Recall@5 0.4133, NDCG 0.3611, 496.05 ms
- bm25l_only: s@1 0.2667, MRR 0.3313, Recall@5 0.4533, NDCG 0.3614, 0.04 ms
- fts_bm25l_rrf: s@1 0.2933, MRR 0.3449, Recall@5 0.4400, NDCG 0.3684, 495.18 ms

Interpretation:
- BM25L is a better secondary lexical signal than a primary replacement.
- BM25L helps surface additional relevant documents, but it also displaces some strong FTS top hits.
- RRF fusion recovers some of the BM25L recall gain while preserving most of FTS5's ranking quality.

Where BM25L helped:
- Cenvat credit disputes with commissioner/location detail.
- Some customs-dispute lookups.
- Some generic tax addition/disallowance queries where FTS5 scattered across similar tax cases.

Where BM25L hurt:
- Bail/FEMA/PMLA lookups where FTS5 already had a precise title/party match.
- Refund claim disputes with strong exact lexical anchors.
- Some income-tax appeal queries with specific party/office phrasing.
- Section 138 / cheque dishonour remains highly ambiguous for both rankers because many cases share nearly identical wording.

Query-level examples:
- Better with BM25L:
  - 176524-3: "cenvat credit dispute commissioner of c. ex., cus. & s.t., tirupati"
    - FTS RR: 0.3333
    - BM25L RR: 1.0
  - 176521-3: "customs dispute case g & s international"
    - FTS RR: 0.25
    - BM25L RR: 0.3333
- Worse with BM25L:
  - 176295-3: "regular bail pmla case rakesh manekchand kothari"
    - FTS RR: 1.0
    - BM25L RR: 0.5
  - 176527-1: "refund claim dispute"
    - FTS RR: 1.0
    - BM25L RR: 0.5
  - 176531-3: "income tax appeal case deputy commissioner of income-tax"
    - FTS RR: 1.0
    - BM25L RR: 0.2

Per-topic top-1 pattern:
- Section 138: tie on success@1 between FTS5, BM25L, and fusion.
- Bail: FTS5 better than BM25L; fusion falls back to FTS-level.
- Cenvat credit: BM25L better than FTS5.
- Refund: FTS5 better than BM25L; fusion falls back to FTS-level.
- Customs: tie on success@1, BM25L sometimes helps rank relevant docs slightly higher.
- Income tax: FTS5 better than BM25L.

Decision:
- Do not replace FTS5 with BM25L.
- If BM25L is used, use it as a secondary lexical ranker or recall augmenter.
- Do not add BM25L to the production app yet without a cleaner retrieval path and a more representative mixed-domain benchmark.

Best next step:
- Inspect a larger sample of FTS vs BM25L disagreements.
- If the disagreements consistently help in non-legal corpora too, consider an optional lexical fusion mode:
  - FTS5 first for top precision
  - BM25L as secondary candidate source
  - RRF only when the FTS result set is weak
